The Team

Governance Infrastructure Requires Technical Depth

Axiosky is founded by operators who understand the gap between AI capability and organizational deployability. We've seen compliance teams block promising pilots, legal teams refuse sign-off on autonomous systems, and auditors fail implementations for lack of provable governance.

We're building the infrastructure layer that solves this: deterministic policy enforcement, architectural governance, and forensic-grade audit trails for regulated environments.

Why This Team Structure

Governance infrastructure is technical architecture work, not traditional product development. It requires:

Deep technical expertise in distributed systems, policy evaluation, and deterministic execution—not broad product management.

Regulatory and compliance domain knowledge built through direct engagement with government procurement officers, bank compliance teams, and healthcare legal counsel—not market research.

Patient, deliberate engineering where correctness matters more than velocity, and certification matters more than features.

This work is done by engineers who understand both the technical architecture and the regulatory constraints. We stay small and focused rather than building a traditional org chart.

Founding Team

Operators with direct experience building systems that survive regulatory scrutiny.

Founder & Chief Architect

Systems Engineer & Governance Architect

Background

Deep experience in policy enforcement systems, distributed architectures, and regulatory compliance automation. Built Axiosky's core platform—Governor, Policy Engine, and Orchestrator—from first principles to solve the fundamental problem: autonomous systems need provable governance to be deployable in regulated environments.

What I'm Building

The technical architecture that makes AI agents certifiable: pre-execution policy enforcement, deterministic compliance logic, and immutable audit trails. Every design decision prioritizes correctness, auditability, and regulatory defensibility over features or speed.

Philosophy

Governance can't be added to autonomous systems—it must be architectural. I'm building infrastructure where enforcement is impossible to bypass, policies are provably correct, and audit trails survive legal scrutiny. This is infrastructure work, not application work.

Role

Strategic support, operational resilience, and maintaining conviction during the long build cycles that deep infrastructure requires.

Why This Matters

Building governance infrastructure means moving deliberately—months of policy codification, regulatory mapping, and certification work before any customer deployment. Having someone who believes in the mission when progress feels slow is what makes long-term technical work sustainable.

How We Build

Engineering principles that drive our development.

Policy Codification Through Direct Engagement

We don't abstract regulations into requirements documents. We work directly with your compliance teams, map regulatory text to enforceable logic, write policy code, and validate with your legal counsel. This requires deep domain engagement, not scaled product teams.

Architecture-First, Not Feature-First

Governor's enforcement isn't a feature you can bypass—it's architectural. Every agent action routes through governance by design, not configuration. This requires systems thinking, not sprint planning.

Certification-Grade Engineering

Every policy rule has unit tests proving correctness. Every decision is replayable. Every audit record is immutable. This level of rigor requires engineering discipline, not rapid iteration.

Our Commitment
