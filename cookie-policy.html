Cookie Policy

Effective Date: 10 March 2026
Last Updated: 10 March 2026

1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies and similar technologies are, how Axiosky uses them on axiosky.com and related pages (the "Site"), and how you can control them.

This policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy, which explains how we handle personal data more broadly.

2. What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. Cookies can help websites function properly, remember preferences, and understand usage patterns.

We may also use similar technologies (such as pixels, tags, or local storage) for similar purposes.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Cookies generally fall into these categories:

  • Strictly necessary cookies — required for core site functionality and security.
  • Functional cookies — remember choices and preferences (where applicable).
  • Analytics cookies — help us understand how the Site is used so we can improve it.
  • Marketing cookies — used to measure or support advertising and retargeting (if enabled).

4. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to operate the Site, improve performance, prevent abuse, and understand which pages and features are most useful to visitors.

Some cookies may be set by us (first-party cookies) and some may be set by third parties whose services we use (third-party cookies).

5. Cookie Details (Declaration)

Because our Site may change over time, the list of cookies may change. Below is a basic disclosure format you can keep updated.

Cookie / Identifier Provider Category Purpose Expiry
(Example) session_id Axiosky Strictly necessary Maintains session state and security. Session
(Example) analytics_id (Your analytics provider) Analytics Measures traffic and usage patterns. (e.g., 13 months)

6. Third-Party Cookies

If we use third-party services (for example, analytics, video embeds, or social media features), those providers may set cookies or similar technologies on your device.

Third parties may process data according to their own privacy policies. We recommend reviewing those policies when relevant.

7. Managing Cookies

You can control cookies in several ways:

  • Browser settings — most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies, and to control cookie preferences.
  • Device controls — your device may provide controls for certain storage or tracking technologies.
  • Cookie banner / preference center — if we implement a cookie banner or preference center, you can use it to manage non-essential cookies.

Please note that blocking strictly necessary cookies may impact Site functionality.

8. Retention

Some cookies are deleted when you close your browser ("session" cookies). Others remain for longer ("persistent" cookies) and expire after a set period unless you delete them earlier.

9. Changes to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. The updated version will be posted on this page with a new "Last Updated" date.
